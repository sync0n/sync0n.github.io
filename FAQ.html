  • Employee cannot login to myKN

    In case employee cannot log into myKN, please check the following: 

    • whether he/she uses correct URL: connections.mykn.community
    • whether he/she logs in with his/her Windows user name and password (the same as he/she would log in to his/her PC). In most cases the login name is firstname.lastname and the personal password
    • the user should also be in Kuehne + Nagel corporate network, either LAN or WLAN or VPN (if the user can open myKN login page, he/she most likely is in corporate network).

  • Employees left KN but are shown as active users

    In some cases employees that left KN might still be shown as active users in myKN / IBM Sametime. If that is the case, then please verify that local HR updated users PDM profile, and notified local LAN support about the fact that employee left KN. Once local LAN support receives a notification (from HR) that user left KN, his/her Active Directory profile will be "deactivated" (timeframes vary by region). and users profile will no longer be shown as active in myKN.

  • How to create a myKN community

    Intro

     

    Community creation process is easy and quick, below are two steps that you need to take in order to set it up:

     

    Pick the community name

     

    We would like to clarify the reasoning behind the community name requirements. We would like all the communities to share a similar name structure. This will be beneficial for users, and community owners, as it will simplify the community search process. It makes it easier for the employees to navigate between communities, understand what different communities we have across the organization, and ultimately, foster cooperation between different teams and units. 

    Please note: community name should be in English, and abbreviations are not allowed. 

    Here is the community name structure that we are looking for: 

    1.Business unit name (without abbreviations, in English) 
    2.The purpose of the community (optional)
    3.Geographical reach of the community 

    Example: 

    Airfreight (< - Business Unit name)  Systems - Business Process Automation (< - The purpose of the community) Global (< - Geographical reach of the community). 

    Result:  Airfreight Systems - Business Process Automation  Global


    You can check the Community Naming Convention here: 

    https://wiki.int.kn/display/MYKN/myKN+-+Community+Owners#myKN-CommunityOwners-NamingConventionforCommunities 

    Once you have composed the community name, it's time to get to the next step:

     

    Request the community to be setup

  • Adding a video to your post

    In order to add a video to a page / app please take the following steps: 

    • Upload a video into your personal / community files folder. 
    • Choose the page / app where  you wish to add a video. It can be added in any app that is using the Rich Content editor (with enabled HTML Source view).
    • Add the following code into the HTML Source of the Rich Content editor: <video width="320" height="240" controls="" dir="ltr">   
      <source src="Link To Your Video" type="video/mp4">  
      </video>  
    • Within the code that you added in the third step replace the text in bold with the link that you copied from the video frame. 
    • Save the changes.

     

    Note: please use videos in .mov format, other formats might fail to play the video for users that use certain browsers. 

    if you are adding a video from your personal files into a community, then make sure that during the video file upload you have chosen to Share with everyone in my organization, otherwise users wont be able to play the video.

  • How to Clear your Web-Browsers cache

    Clearing the browsers cache can solve a variety of issues such as performance, functionality of web pages and even login issues.

    Please keep in mind that the supported browsers for the myKN platform are Internet Explorer and Mozilla Firefox, as such we would recommend using one of them to achieve optimal performance and functionality of the platform.

    How to clear the cache for Internet Explorer :

    Step 1
    Navigate to the top right section of the browsers where you will see a "Gear" icon and select "Internet Options" from there.





    Step 2
    This will open up the General tab by default, from there click on "Delete..." which is located in the "Browsing History" section.





    Step 3
    A pop-up will appear with additional options, make sure that the following boxes are ticked: Preserve Favorites website data, Temporary Internet files and website files, Cookies and website data, Form Data, Passwords.
    Once the boxes are accordingly ticked simply click on "Delete" and your Internet Explorer's cache is cleared.





    How to clear the cache for Mozilla Firefox :

    Step 1
    In the top right section of the browser there is a "Burger" icon, from there select "Options".





    Step 2
    From the browser settings screen navigate to "Privacy & Security", locate the "Cookies and Site Data" section and press "Clear Data...". Now your Mozilla Firefox cache is cleared.


  • How To Create an RSS Feed for Outlook

    Creating an RSS Feed for Outlook would be particularly useful if you want to receive updates to your Outlook about notifications for a specific app or a communities updates. Naturally the same can be done for a news article on the web, but let's focus on why it is good to have an RSS Feed for apps in myKN and why would you want to do that.

    The notification settings which you can adjust from your personal profile are Global settings, what that means is if you would choose to get emails about updates then you would automatically get a notification e-mail into your inbox about every update for every community that you are following. If you are a part of multiple communities this can cause a lot of clutter and traffic for your inbox and would make it a fair deal harder to navigate it. By creating an RSS Feed you have the possibility to choose what kind of notifications and from where you receive them. 

    The receive all of the updates from a community we would recommend creating a feed for the "Recent Updates" and "Status Updates" apps of the desired community. If you would like to receive updates only for a specific forum which you found to be of interest, use or importance, you can do so and at the same time not receive notification emails about what else goes on in the community in general.

    An example then, let's say that community "X" in general does not have much value for me individually in my day to day work, but I found some forum posts/blog posts that are directly related to my daily work and they also post various announcements which are of great use to me. Then I would create an RSS Feed for that forum/blog and receive notifications to a standalone RSS Feed folder, allowing me to optimize the relevance of notifications that I receive as well as organizing it. Instead of getting an e-mail for every community that has an update and I am following.

    The process of creating an RSS Feed for Outlook is fairly short. There are two ways to create an RSS Feed for Outlook:

     

    1. Option:

    First open up the app that you would like to create an RSS Feed for and scroll to the bottom of it, once there you will see the RSS Feed icon with the available options, for some apps you can just create a feed for comments or all entries, for some you can just create a feed for entries.

     

     

    Once you click on it, it will open up a new page with the feed itself, now you would need to copy the entire URL of the page that opened, this is located at the top section of the web-browser.

     

     

    Once the entire URL is copied, open up your Outlook, right click on "RSS Feeds" folder located on the left side, from the available options select "Add a New RSS Feed...".

    If this is your first time creating an RSS Feed for Outlook you would need to navigate to the "File" tab which is located in the top left, click on "Account Settings", this will open up a menu, from there click on "Account Settings...". Once the account settings window pops-up navigate to the "RSS Feeds" tab and click on "New...". Now you would have to paste the copied URL into the box and click "Add" and "OK" afterwards. This process will come up with a prompt requesting your windows credentials, type them in and make sure to have outlook save your credentials so that you could use the shorter and easier way of creating an RSS Feed in the future.

     

     

    This will give you a pop-up where you would need to paste the URL we copied earlier and then click "Add" once done. A new pop-up will appear asking for confirmation if you want to add this feed to Outlook, click "Yes" and you have successfully created an RSS Feed for Outlook.

     

     

    2. Option:

    1. Open up the files app and click on "Feed for this Folder"

     

     

    2. Copy the link

     

    3. Go to Outlook and click on File -> Info -> Account Settings

     

    4. Click in the tab "RSS Feeds" on "New" and add the link

     

    5. A second window appears. Confirm with "ok".
